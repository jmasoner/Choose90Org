# Alternative Words to "Pledge" - Brainstorming

## The Problem
"Pledge" can sound:
- Too serious/formal
- Like a financial commitment
- Like a legal obligation
- Intimidating or scary

## Goal
Find words that are:
- ✅ Friendly and approachable
- ✅ Don't sound like they cost money
- ✅ Don't seem like spam/scam
- ✅ Still convey commitment/participation
- ✅ Action-oriented

---

## Top Recommendations

### 1. **"Join"** ⭐ (Best Overall)
**Examples:**
- "Join Choose90"
- "Join the Movement"
- "Join Us"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Simple, friendly, welcoming
- ✅ No cost implications
- ✅ Clear action
- ✅ Inclusive feeling
- ✅ Works everywhere (buttons, pages, CTAs)

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Might be too generic
- ⚠️ Doesn't emphasize the commitment aspect

**Best for:** Buttons, main CTAs, navigation

---

### 2. **"Get Started"** ⭐ (Great for CTAs)
**Examples:**
- "Get Started with Choose90"
- "Start Your Journey"
- "Get Started Today"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Action-oriented
- ✅ Friendly and approachable
- ✅ No commitment pressure
- ✅ Forward-looking, positive
- ✅ Works well for onboarding

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Doesn't emphasize the community aspect
- ⚠️ Might sound like a product signup

**Best for:** Hero sections, landing pages, first-time visitors

---

### 3. **"Join the Movement"** ⭐ (Inspiring)
**Examples:**
- "Join the Choose90 Movement"
- "Join the Movement"
- "Be Part of the Movement"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Inspiring and empowering
- ✅ Emphasizes community
- ✅ Clear purpose
- ✅ Not scary or formal
- ✅ Makes people feel part of something bigger

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Slightly longer (might not fit all buttons)
- ⚠️ "Movement" might sound political to some

**Best for:** Page titles, descriptions, marketing copy

---

### 4. **"Take the Challenge"**
**Examples:**
- "Take the Choose90 Challenge"
- "Start the Challenge"
- "Join the Challenge"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Engaging and gamified
- ✅ Clear action
- ✅ Fun, not scary
- ✅ Implies it's achievable

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ "Challenge" might sound like work
- ⚠️ Might intimidate some people
- ⚠️ Less about commitment, more about activity

**Best for:** Campaign pages, challenge-specific content

---

### 5. **"Be Part of Choose90"**
**Examples:**
- "Be Part of Choose90"
- "Be Part of It"
- "Be Part of the Community"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Warm and inclusive
- ✅ Emphasizes belonging
- ✅ Friendly tone
- ✅ No cost implications

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Slightly passive ("be" vs "join")
- ⚠️ Longer for buttons

**Best for:** Descriptions, about sections

---

### 6. **"Start Choosing 90"**
**Examples:**
- "Start Choosing 90"
- "Start Choosing Positive"
- "Choose 90 Today"

**Pros:**
- ✅ Uses your brand name
- ✅ Action-oriented
- ✅ Clear what it's about
- ✅ Memorable

**Cons:**
- ⚠️ Might be confusing if people don't know what "90" means
- ⚠️ Less about joining, more about action

**Best for:** Branded campaigns, when brand is established

---

## Context-Specific Recommendations

### For Navigation/Buttons:
1. **"Join"** - Short, clear, friendly
2. **"Get Started"** - Action-oriented
3. **"Join Us"** - Welcoming

### For Page Titles:
1. **"Join the Choose90 Movement"** - Inspiring
2. **"Get Started with Choose90"** - Friendly
3. **"Join Choose90"** - Simple

### For Hero Sections:
1. **"Join the Movement"** - Empowering
2. **"Get Started Today"** - Urgent but friendly
3. **"Be Part of Something Positive"** - Inspiring

### For Email/Newsletter:
1. **"Join Choose90"** - Clear
2. **"Get Started"** - Action-oriented
3. **"Be Part of the Community"** - Warm

---

## Words to AVOID

❌ **"Sign Up"** - Sounds like spam/email list
❌ **"Register"** - Too formal, sounds like a service
❌ **"Commit"** - Too serious, like "pledge"
❌ **"Enroll"** - Sounds like a course/cost
❌ **"Subscribe"** - Sounds like paid subscription
❌ **"Become a Member"** - Sounds like a paid membership
❌ **"Join Now"** - The "now" can sound pushy

---

## Recommended Strategy

### Primary: Use **"Join"**
- Simple, friendly, clear
- Works everywhere
- No negative connotations
- Easy to understand

### Secondary: Use **"Get Started"** for CTAs
- More action-oriented
- Great for first-time visitors
- Implies beginning a journey

### For Descriptions: Use **"Join the Movement"**
- More inspiring
- Emphasizes community
- Makes it feel bigger than just signing up
