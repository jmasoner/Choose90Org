Local Good Directory Template

Create a hyper-local resource of positive things

Why a Local Good Directory?

Your community is full of good things: businesses doing right by their employees, community gardens, volunteer opportunities, gathering places, people making a difference. But we often don't know about them.

This template helps you create a living directory of the good in your community. Share it with neighbors. Keep it updated. Watch it grow.

📋 How to Use This Template

  1. Start small. Add 5-10 things you already know about.
  2. Share it. Post it in your neighborhood group, share with friends.
  3. Ask for contributions. "What good things should we add?"
  4. Keep it updated. Add new discoveries. Remove things that are no longer active.
  5. Make it accessible. Keep it in a shared document, website, or printed version.

💡 Tips for Building Your Directory

  • Start with what you know. Don't wait until it's perfect. Start with 5 things and grow.
  • Ask around. "What good things are happening in our community?"
  • Verify information. Make sure contact info and details are current.
  • Get permission. Before listing someone, ask if it's okay.
  • Keep it positive. This is about the good, not complaints.
  • Make it accessible. Share it widely. Keep it updated.
  • Celebrate additions. When someone adds something, thank them.

📱 Digital Options

Consider creating your directory as:

  • Google Doc/Sheet: Easy to share and collaborate
  • Simple Website: More permanent, easier to find
  • Neighborhood App: Nextdoor, Facebook group, etc.
  • Printed Version: For those who prefer paper, update quarterly

Ready to Start?

Fill in what you know. Share it. Ask others to contribute. Watch your directory of good grow.
